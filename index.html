The Classic PBJ

First referenced in 1901 by Julia Davis Chandler, the PBJ sandwich was an immediate hit with the youths and has remained a favorite since. Peanut butter was considered a bit of a delicacy back then and the PBJ made its first appearances in tea rooms and upscale settings. Eventually, with the commercialization of peanut butter, lower classes were able to afford the sticky substance. In the 1920s, sliced bread was invented which allowed children to make the sandwiches themselves without the need for a sharp knife and the rest, as they say, is history. The ease with which the sandwich could be constructed allowed it to become a major meal for children during the Great Depression. You can learn More at the History of the PBJ

Children, it seems, are not the only ones known to enjoy this Classic Sandwich. Musicans are often tied to this sandwich. According to an article for 2011 by the Lockport Journal of New York, several musicians preformed for their local "Peanut Butter and Jelly Drive". The drive aims to fill 3 counties worth of food pantries with the lacking peanut butter and jelly. The Peanut Butter and Jelly Drive of Lockport celebrated its 9th year in 2018.

Here's how you can make your own

  1. Gather necessary supplies. You'll need two slices of bread, a jar of jam or jelly and a jar of peanut butter. You might also want to grab a knife or a spoon.
  2. Slather peanut butter on one slice of bread.
  3. Spread your jam on the second slice.
  4. Press the slices together so that the peanut butter and jelly meet in the middle.
  5. That's it! You're done!! Enjoy that glorious sandwich.
